## Purpose

The **Load and Trim Sheet** is the legal record of the aircraft's mass and balance calculation for a specific flight. It serves two main purposes:
1.  **Mass Check**: Ensures MTOM, MLM, and MZFM limits are respected.
2.  **Balance Check**: Ensures the CG is within limits and determines the take-off trim setting.

## Sections of the Sheet

### 1. Header
Flight number, date, registration, crew configuration (e.g., 2/4), and airport codes.

### 2. Loading Distribution
Breakdown of passengers and cargo in each zone/hold.
*   **Traffic Load**: Sum of all passengers, bags, and cargo.

### 3. Mass Build-up
*   **Dry Operating Mass (DOM)**
*   **+ Traffic Load**
*   **= Zero Fuel Mass (ZFM)** (Check against MZFM limit)
*   **+ Take-off Fuel**
*   **= Take-Off Mass (TOM)** (Check against MTOM and Performance/Runway limits)
*   **- Trip Fuel**
*   **= Estimated Landing Mass (ELM)** (Check against MLM)

### 4. Trim Calculation (Balance)
Uses an index system (graphical "drop line" or numerical table) to calculate the final Index or %MAC.
*   **Result**: The Take-off CG position (usually in %MAC).
*   **Action**: Determines the **THS (Trimmable Horizontal Stabilizer)** or Trim Wheel setting required for take-off (e.g., "4.5 Units UP" or "28%").

## Commander's Signature
The commander (captain) must sign the load sheet, accepting responsibility that the loading is within limits.
