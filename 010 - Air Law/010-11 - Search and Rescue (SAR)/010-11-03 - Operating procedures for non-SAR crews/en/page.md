## Interception of Distress Signals
If a pilot-in-command intercepts a distress transmission and is not a dedicated SAR aircraft, they should:
1.  **Acknowledge** the distress transmission (if feasible).
2.  **Record the position** of the craft in distress.
3.  Take a bearing on the transmission.
4.  Inform the appropriate RCC or ATS unit.
5.  At discretion, proceed to the position (while awaiting instructions).

## At the Accident Scene
If the first aircraft to reach the scene **is not a SAR aircraft**:
*   It should take charge of on-scene activities (On-Scene Commander) until a dedicated SAR aircraft arrives or control is transferred to another aircraft with better communications.
