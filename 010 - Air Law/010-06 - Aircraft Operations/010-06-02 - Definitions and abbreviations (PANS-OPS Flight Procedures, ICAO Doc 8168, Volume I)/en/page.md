# PANS-OPS Definitions and Abbreviations: Protection Areas, Segments, and Tolerances

This section introduces key concepts from ICAO Document 8168 (PANS-OPS), fundamental for the design of instrument flight procedures.

## Areas and Margins

*   **Protection Areas**: When designing a procedure, the vertical airspace of a segment is divided into:
    *   **Primary Area**: Central zone located symmetrically on both sides of the track.
        *   On straight segments, its width is **half of the total width**.
        *   In turns without track guidance, **the total width** is considered primary area.
        *   **Full Minimum Obstacle Clearance (MOC)** is provided across its entire width.
    *   **Secondary Area**: Outer lateral zones.
        *   Obstacle clearance reduces linearly **from the primary area margin to zero** at the outer edge.
        *   On straight segments, each secondary area has a width of **one quarter of the total**.

## Maneuver Definitions

*   **Circling Approach (Visual Maneuvering)**: It is the phase after completing an instrument approach that positions the aircraft for landing on a runway **not suitable for a straight-in approach** (due to excessive alignment or descent gradient).
*   **Final Approach Segment (FAS)**: Segment where alignment and descent for landing are performed.
*   **DER (Departure End of Runway)**: The end of the runway available for the ground run of an aircraft taking off (opposite to the landing threshold).

## Tolerance Factors

Several factors affect the accuracy of a fix and its associated tolerance area:
*   **Airborne receiver tolerance**: Error in aircraft equipment (e.g., VOR, ADF).
*   **Ground station tolerance**: Margin of error of the transmitting station.
*   **Flight technical tolerance**: Accuracy with which the aircraft is piloted, display errors, etc.
*   *Note: Meteorological tolerance is not considered a direct factor in the geometric accuracy of the fix.*

## Aircraft Categories

The most significant factor affecting the airspace required and visibility for instrument maneuvers is the **aircraft speed**.
