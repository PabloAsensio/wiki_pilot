# Global Aviation Organizations: ICAO, EASA, and IATA Roles

In international civil aviation, several organizations play key roles, representing different interests and establishing global standards.

## International Air Transport Association (IATA)

**IATA** is the trade association representing the world's airlines. It comprises some 290 airlines, representing approximately 82% of total air traffic.

### Mission and Objectives
IATA's main mission is to **represent, lead, and serve the airline industry**. Its objectives include:
*   Promoting safety, reliability, and economy in air transport.
*   Improving understanding of the air transport industry among decision makers.
*   Advocating for the interests of airlines by challenging unreasonable rules and charges.
*   Helping to **formulate industry policies** on critical aviation issues.

Unlike ICAO, which is a forum of States, IATA is a private organization of air operators.

## Key Differences Between Organizations

*   **ICAO:** A UN specialized agency serving as a global forum for **States**. Its mission is to develop policies, standards (SARPs), and audit compliance for international civil aviation.
*   **EASA:** The European Union Aviation Safety Agency, whose mission is to promote the highest common standards of safety and environmental protection in civil aviation at the European level. It develops common rules (Hard Law and Soft Law).
*   **IATA:** Represents **airlines** (private sector) and seeks to serve their commercial and operational interests.
