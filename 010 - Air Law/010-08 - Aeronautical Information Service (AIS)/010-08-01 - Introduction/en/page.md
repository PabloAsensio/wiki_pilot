# AIS Introduction (Annex 15): Definition, State Responsibilities, and Service Availability

## Definition and Object (Annex 15)
The **Aeronautical Information Service (AIS)** is the service established to provide aeronautical data and aeronautical information necessary for the:
*   **Safety**.
*   **Regularity**.
*   **Efficiency** of international air navigation.

## State Responsibilities (Annex 15, Chap 3)
Each Contracting State is responsible for:
1.  Providing an AIS itself.
2.  Agreeing with another State to provide a joint service.
3.  Delegating the authority to a non-governmental agency (provided standards are met).

The AIS must receive, collate, edit, format, publish, and distribute information concerning **the entire territory of the State** and areas where it is responsible for air traffic services (e.g., high seas).

## Service Availability
*   If not a 24-hour service, it must be available during the entire period an aircraft is in flight in its area of responsibility, plus **2 hours before and after**.
