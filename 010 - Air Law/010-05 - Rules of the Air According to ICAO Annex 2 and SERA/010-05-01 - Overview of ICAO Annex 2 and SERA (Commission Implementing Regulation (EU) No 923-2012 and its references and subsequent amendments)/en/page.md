# ICAO Annex 2 and SERA Overview: Scope, Definitions, and Aerodrome Operations

Commission Implementing Regulation (EU) No 923/2012, known as **SERA (Standardised European Rules of the Air)**, establishes common rules of the air and operational provisions regarding services and procedures in air navigation.

## Objective and Scope
The objective of SERA is to apply common rules to general air traffic. It applies to:
1.  Airspace users and aircraft operating **into, within, or out of the Union**.
2.  Aircraft bearing the **nationality and registration marks of a Member State** of the Union, operating in any airspace (provided they do not conflict with the rules of the country overflown).

## Relationship with ICAO Annex 2
SERA implements ICAO Annex 2 standards into European legislation.
*   **Over the High Seas:** The rules of ICAO Annex 2 apply without exception.
*   **Over National Territory:** The rules published by the State having jurisdiction over the territory overflown take precedence.

## Important Definitions
*   **Flight Visibility:** The visibility forward from the cockpit of an aircraft in flight.
*   **Ground Visibility:** The visibility at an aerodrome as reported by an accredited observer or by automatic systems.
*   **Danger Area:** An airspace of defined dimensions within which activities dangerous to the flight of aircraft may exist at specified times.
*   **Restricted Area:** An airspace of defined dimensions, above the land areas or territorial waters of a State, within which the flight of aircraft is restricted in accordance with certain specified conditions.

## Operation at Aerodromes
An aircraft operating on or in the vicinity of an aerodrome shall:
*   Observe other traffic for the purpose of **avoiding collision**.
*   Conform to or avoid the pattern of traffic formed by other aircraft in operation.
*   Make all **turns to the left** when approaching for a landing and after taking off, unless otherwise indicated.
*   Land and take off **into the wind**, unless safety or runway configuration determine otherwise.
