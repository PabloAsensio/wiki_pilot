# Response Management to Unlawful Interference: Hijacking and Commander Powers

## Response to Unlawful Seizure (Hijacking)
When an aircraft subjected to an act of unlawful seizure lands in a Contracting State:

*   **Detention:** The State shall detain the aircraft on the ground **unless** its departure is necessitated by the overriding duty to protect human life.
*   **Assistance:** The State shall provide:
    *   Air navigation aids.
    *   Air traffic services (ATC).
    *   Permission to land.

## Tokyo Convention (Powers of the Aircraft Commander)
*   The aircraft commander may disembark any person who he believes has committed or is about to commit an act of unlawful interference.
*   May deliver to the competent authorities any person who has committed a serious act.
