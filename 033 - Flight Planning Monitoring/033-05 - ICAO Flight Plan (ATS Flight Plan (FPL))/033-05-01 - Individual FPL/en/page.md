## Purpose
The FPL provides ATS with information regarding the intended flight for safety (SAR) and traffic management. It must be submitted at least **60 mins** before departure (or 3 hours for flow control areas).

## Key Fields
*   **Item 7 (Callsign)**: Aircraft identification.
*   **Item 8 (Rules)**: I (IFR), V (VFR), Y (IFR to VFR), Z (VFR to IFR).
*   **Item 9 (Type & Wake)**: E.g., B738/M (Medium).
*   **Item 10 (Equipment)**:
    *   **Radio**: S (Standard: VHF, VOR, ILS), G (GNSS), R (PBN), Y (8.33 kHz radio).
    *   **Surveillance**: C (Mode C Transponder), S (Mode S), B1 (ADS-B Out).
*   **Item 13 (Dep/Time)**: 4-letter ICAO code and EOBT (Estimated Off-Block Time) in UTC.
*   **Item 15 (Route)**: Cruising speed, flight level, and route defined by waypoints/airways.
*   **Item 16 (Dest/Time/Alt)**: Destination, EET (Estimated Elapsed Time), and Alternates.
*   **Item 18 (Other Info)**:
    *   **PBN/**: Performance Based Navigation capabilities (e.g., PBN/A1B1).
    *   **REG/**: Registration if different from callsign.
    *   **OPR/**: Operator.
    *   **RMK/**: Remarks.

## Delays
If the flight is delayed by more than **30 minutes** (for controlled flights) or **60 minutes** (for uncontrolled), the FPL must be amended or cancelled and resubmitted (specific rules vary by region).
