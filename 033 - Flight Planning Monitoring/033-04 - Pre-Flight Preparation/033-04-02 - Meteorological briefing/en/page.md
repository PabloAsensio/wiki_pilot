## Meteorological Reports (Actual Weather)

*   **METAR**: Meteorological Terminal Air Report. Routine observation (usually every 30 or 60 mins).
*   **SPECI**: Special report issued when significant changes occur between routine METARs.

## Meteorological Forecasts

*   **TAF (Terminal Aerodrome Forecast)**: Forecast for a specific airport (Validity usually 9, 24, or 30 hours).
    *   Essential for checking Destination and Alternate suitability.
*   **TREND**: A short landing forecast appended to a METAR (valid for 2 hours).

## En-Route Weather

*   **SIGMET (Significant Meteorological Information)**: Warning of severe weather (Turbulence, Icing, Ash, Thunderstorms) affecting all aircraft.
*   **AIRMET**: Warning of weather potentially hazardous to low-level aircraft (smaller than SIGMET).
*   **Significant Weather Charts (SIGWX)**: Graphic charts showing fronts, jet streams, tropopause height, and turbulence areas.
*   **Wind and Temperature Charts**: Show wind direction/speed and temperature at various Flight Levels. Used for calculating Ground Speed and Fuel Temp.

## Briefing Sources
*   **Self-briefing**: Using automated systems.
*   **Consultation**: Asking a meteorologist (less common now).
*   **Documentation**: Charts and reports provided in the flight folder.
