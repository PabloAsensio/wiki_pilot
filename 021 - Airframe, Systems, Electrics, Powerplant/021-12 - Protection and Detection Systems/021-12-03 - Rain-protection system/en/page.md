# Aircraft Rain Protection Systems: Wipers, Repellents, and Hydrophobic Coatings

## Overview
Rain protection systems are critical for maintaining pilot visibility during taxi, takeoff, approach, and landing in wet conditions.

## 1. Windshield Wipers
*   **Description**: Electro-mechanical arms with blades, similar to car wipers but robust enough for high speeds.
*   **Operation**:
    *   Usually controlled independently for the Captain and First Officer.
    *   Can have multiple speeds (Low/High).
*   **Limitations**:
    *   Can be noisy.
    *   Efficiency decreases at very high airspeeds.
*   **Constraint**: Should **not** be operated on a dry windshield (causes scratching/damage).

## 2. Chemical Rain Repellent
*   **Description**: A fluid (hydrophobic chemical) sprayed onto the windshield from a reservoir.
*   **Mechanism**: Reduces the surface tension of water, causing it to bead up and be blown off by the airflow.
*   **Operational Rules**:
    *   **MUST ONLY** be applied in **moderate to heavy rain**.
    *   **NEVER** apply to a dry windshield.
        *   **Reason**: The fluid is viscous and will dry as a smear/stain, severely restricting visibility.
        *   **Recovery**: If momentarily applied to a dry window, **DO NOT use wipers**. Wipers will smear it further. It requires rain or water to wash off.

## 3. Hydrophobic Seal Coatings
*   **Description**: A permanent or semi-permanent coating applied to the glass surface by the manufacturer or maintenance.
*   **Mechanism**: Penetrates the surface to provide a long-lasting hydrophobic (water-repelling) effect. Rain beads up and falls off naturally.
*   **Comparison**:
    *   **Pros**: Lasts much longer than chemical repellents; passive system (no pilot action required).
    *   **Cons**: Re-application/restoration requires a maintenance procedure (more effort than refilling a fluid tank).
*   **Note**: It reduces the reliance on wipers but **does not replace** them.
