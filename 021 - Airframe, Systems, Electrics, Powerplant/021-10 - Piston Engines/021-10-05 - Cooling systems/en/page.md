### Air Cooling Principles

*   **Mechanism**: Reciprocating engines (especially in GA) are typically **air-cooled**.
    *   Uses **Ram Air** from the atmosphere to cool cylinders.
    *   **Source**: Propeller slipstream and aircraft forward movement.
*   **Cylinder Fins**:
    *   Cylinders have external **fins** to increase the surface area in contact with the cooling air.
    *   Improving heat transfer.
*   **Cowling**: Used to direct airflow efficiently over all cylinders (front and rear) and reduce drag.

### Cowl Flaps

*   **Definition**: Movable flaps (usually at the bottom/rear of the engine cowling) that regulate the amount of air flowing through the engine compartment.
*   **Function**: Control engine temperature by regulating airflow.
    *   **Open**: Increases airflow $\rightarrow$ **Decreases** engine temperature. Used during high power/low airspeed operations (e.g., Takeoff, Climb).
    *   **Closed**: Decreases airflow $\rightarrow$ Increases engine temperature (or prevents over-cooling). Used during low power/high airspeed operations (e.g., Descent, Cruise) to prevent **shock cooling** and reduce drag.
