**Introduction**

Aircraft structures are assembled using various attachment methods to join components securely. The integrity of these attachments is vital for airworthiness. Two common methods discussed are bonding and riveting.

## Bonding

Bonding is an attachment process where a relatively light material is added at the joining surfaces of components.

- **Process:** The material changes its physical state as it dries and solidifies, creating a **chemical bond** with each of the elements.
- **Defects:** Cracks may develop due to unequal loading forces or missing bolts.
- **Visual Inspection:** Issues may be visually identified by observing missing rivets, joints, or collision damages.

## Rivets

Rivets are a widely used method for attaching materials together, such as the skin of an airframe. They work by inserting a rivet into holes in the materials to be bonded and deforming the end to hold it in place.

- **Loss of Rivets:** While rivets are generally secure, they can be lost during normal operations. The most likely causes are **improper maintenance** or **improper installation**, which make them insecure.
    - **Note:** Hard landings and high manoeuvring loads can also remove fasteners through shearing forces, but improper maintenance/installation is considered the primary cause. Corrosion is unlikely to remove rivets unless extremely severe.
- **Loose Rivets:** If rivets become loose, a small gap may form between the panel and the structure. In this condition, the **panel is considered partly detached from the structure**.
