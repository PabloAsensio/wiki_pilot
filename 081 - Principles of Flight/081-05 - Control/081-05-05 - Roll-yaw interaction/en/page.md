## Coupled Motion
Roll and Yaw are rarely isolated.
*   **Roll causes Yaw**: Sideslip towards the low wing -> Weathercock stability yaws nose into the slip. Also adverse yaw.
*   **Yaw causes Roll**: Outer wing moves faster -> More Lift -> Rolls into the turn.

## Adverse Yaw (Review)
*   **Cause**: Induced drag differential from aileron deflection.
*   **Effect**: Nose yaws opposite to the turn entry.
*   **Correction**: Coordinated rudder input into the turn.

## Sideslip
Movement of the aircraft where the relative wind is not aligned with the longitudinal axis.
*   **Essential for Crosswind Landings**: Prevents drift while keeping nose aligned (or checking drift).
*   **Induced by**: Rudder input without bank, or bank without turn (slipping turn).
