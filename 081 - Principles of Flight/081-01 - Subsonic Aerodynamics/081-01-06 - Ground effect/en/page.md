# Ground Effect


## Mechanism
When within approximately **one wingspan** height of the surface:
*   The ground interferes with the formation of wing tip vortices.
*   The vortices are weaker.
*   Downwash is reduced.
*   The effective Angle of Attack increases.

## Effects
1.  **Reduced Induced Drag**: The clearer lift vector (less backward tilt) means less induced drag.
2.  **Increased Lift**: For a fixed angle of attack, lift increases ($C_L$ increases).
3.  **Pitch Change**: Downwash change alters the airflow over the tailplane, typically causing a nose-down moment (reduction in downwash on tail).
4.  **Local Static Pressure**: Increases below the wing (cushion effect).

## Consequences for Flight
*   **Landing**: The aircraft tends to "float". If speed is not reduced, the aircraft will glide further than expected.
*   **Take-off**: The aircraft may become airborne before reaching the safe climb speed ($V_2$). If it climbs out of ground effect too early, induced drag will suddenly increase, potentially causing it to sink back down.
