# Reducing CL-CD Ratio


## Purpose
Sometimes pilots need to increase Drag (to descend stepeer or slow down) or destroy Lift (to ensure weight is on wheels during landing).

## Spoilers
Plates on the upper surface of the wing that spoil the smooth airflow.
*   **Flight Spoilers / Speed Brakes**: Can be extended symmetrically in flight.
    *   Increase Drag significantly.
    *   Decrease Lift.
    *   Used for emergency descent or slowing down.
*   **Roll Spoilers**: Operate asymmetrically (one side up) to assist ailerons in banking.
*   **Ground Spoilers (Lift Dumpers)**: Extend fully on touchdown.
    *   Primary purpose: **Destroy Lift**. This puts weight on the wheels, making wheel brakes effective.
    *   Secondary purpose: Increase aerodynamic drag.

## Airbrakes
Dedicated drag-producing surfaces (e.g., tail cone, fuselage panels).
*   Difference from Spoilers: Airbrakes increase Drag **without** significantly affecting Lift (ideally). Spoilers definitely affect Lift.
