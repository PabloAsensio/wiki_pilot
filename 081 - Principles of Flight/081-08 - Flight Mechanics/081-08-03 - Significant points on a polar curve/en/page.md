# Polar Curve Points


## Drag Polar
A graph of Lift Coefficient ($C_L$) vs Drag Coefficient ($C_D$).
*   **Tangent from Origin**: The point of maximum **Lift-to-Drag Ratio** ($L/D_{max}$).
*   **Top of the Curve**: $C_{Lmax}$ (Stall speed).

## Significant Speeds (Thrust Required Curve)
Plotting Drag (Thrust Required) vs Speed ($V$).

1.  **Vmd (Minimum Drag Speed)**: The bottom of the U-shaped curve.
    *   Occurs where Parasite Drag = Induced Drag.
    *   Speed for **Max Range** (Jet) and **Max Endurance** (Prop - approximately).
    *   Speed for **Best Glide Angle** (Max Distance).

2.  **Vmp (Minimum Power Speed)**:
    *   Occurs at approximately $0.76 \times V_{MD}$.
    *   The point where the product of Drag $\times$ Velocity is minimum.
    *   Speed for **Max Endurance** (Jet) because fuel flow is proportional to thrust $\times$ speed (power).
    *   Speed for **Minimum Sink Rate** in a glide.

3.  **Vx (Best Angle of Climb)**: Speed where **Excess Thrust** is maximum. Close to $V_{MD}$.
4.  **Vy (Best Rate of Climb)**: Speed where **Excess Power** is maximum. Higher than $V_x$.
