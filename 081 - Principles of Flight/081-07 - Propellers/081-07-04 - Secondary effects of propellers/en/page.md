# Secondary Propeller Effects


## 1. Torque Reaction
Based on Newton's 3rd Law.
*   **Effect**: The engine turns the prop Right -> The aircraft tends to Roll Left (in the air) or Yaw Left (on the ground due to tire friction difference).

## 2. Slipstream Effect (Corkscrew)
The slipstream spirals around the fuselage.
*   **Effect**: Hits the **Left** side of the vertical fin.
*   **Result**: Pushes tail Right -> Nose Yaws **Left**.
*   **Correction**: Right Rudder (mostly at high power/low speed).

## 3. Gyroscopic Precession
A force applied to a rotating disc is felt 90° later in the direction of rotation.
*   **Tail Up (Takeoff)**: Force applied to TOP of prop arc -> Felt at RIGHT side -> Yaws **Left**.

## 4. Asymmetric Blade Effect (P-Factor)
Occurs at high Angles of Attack (Climb/Takeoff).
*   **Descending Blade (Right side)**: Has higher effective AoA and speed -> Produces MORE Thrust.
*   **Ascending Blade (Left side)**: Has lower AoA -> Produces LESS Thrust.
*   **Result**: Center of Thrust moves to the Right -> Yaws **Left**.

*Note: All examples assume a standard clockwise-rotating propeller (viewed from behind).*
