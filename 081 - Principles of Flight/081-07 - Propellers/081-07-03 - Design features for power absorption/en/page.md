## The Problem
Powerful engines (turboprops) need to convert huge horsepower into thrust without the propeller tips going supersonic (which kills efficiency and creates noise).

## Solutions
1.  **Increase Solidity**: Ratio of blade area to disc area.
    *   **More Blades**: move from 3 to 4, 5, or 6 blades (e.g., C-130J has 6 blades).
    *   **Wider Chord**: Broader blades.
2.  **Scimitar Blades**: Swept-back tips to delay compressibility effects at high tip speeds (like a swept wing).
3.  **Contra-Rotating Propellers**: Two propellers on the same axis rotating in opposite directions.
    *   **Advantage**: Cancels torque, recovers energy from the slipstream (swirl), allows smaller diameter.
    *   **Disadvantage**: Complex, noisy.
