# Maintenance and Monitoring Systems: ACMS Data Use and FDM Safety Analytics

## Aeroplane Condition Monitoring System (ACMS)
**ACMS** (Airbus refers to it as **AIDS** - Aircraft Integrated Data System) monitors aircraft systems to identify maintenance needs and anticipate failures.
*   **Function**: Continuously or periodically measures system parameters (Engines, APU, Flight Controls, etc.).
*   **Transmission**: Data can be transmitted automatically via **Datalink (ACARS)** to the operator's Maintenance Control Center **without crew notification**, or printed on the flight deck.
*   **Goal**: Predictive maintenance and troubleshooting.

## Flight Data Monitoring (FDM)
**FDM** is a systematic method of accessing, analyzing, and acting upon information obtained from digital flight data records of routine daily operations. It is a requirement for operators of aircraft > 27,000 kg.
*   **Purpose**: To improve **Flight Safety** and operational efficiency.
    *   Identify potential risks and adverse safety trends.
    *   Modify and **tailor pilot training programs**.
*   **Principles**:
    *   **Non-Punitive**: Data is used for safety, not discipline.
    *   **Anonymous**: Data is de-identified, except in cases of "grave exceedances" where further investigation is warranted.
